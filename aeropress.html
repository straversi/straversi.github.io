Sometimes I like to enjoy the finer things in life. One of the advantages of coffee is that it's easy to enjoy during times when it's hard to enjoy anything else. Say goodmorning to my favorite aeropress recipes, translated by me for folks like myself who don't want to break out the scales and thermometers for a cup of joe.

Blue Bottle Revisited

What you'll need:

  • A handful of coffee grounds
  • Kettle or water boiler or stove and pan
  • Tap water
  • Your trusty Aeropress

How to do:

  1. Bring your water to a boil somehow.
  2. Invert your aeropress, and measure two scoops of grounds into it.
  3. By now, your water should be a nice "not too hot" temperature. If you're a speedy scooper, chill for a sec. Pour it in your Aeropress to the "Three" mark.
  4. Count to thirty.
  5. Fill your Aeropress with water to the tippy-top.
  6. Stir a couple times.
  7. Count to sixty.
  8. Slap a filter and cap on your Aeropress, flip, press, and enjoy! Mmmmm.

The Rustic Steven

Us MURICANS like ourselves a BIG POT O' COFFEE, none of this dainty half-cup stuff. This recipe accomodates a larger, correct, American cup.

What you'll need:

  • A handful of coffee grounds
  • Kettle or water boiler or stove and pan
  • Tap water
  • Your trusty Aeropress

How to do:

  1. Bring your water to a boil somehow. Let it cool for 5 minutes.
  2. Fill your aeropress with coffee grounds to just above the "1" mark.
  3. Pour the hot water slowly down the side of your Aeropress, to the top.
  4. Insert the plunger at the top and let it all sit for 1 minute. Don't stir yet!
  5. Pull out the plunger and stir gently until satifyingly smooth. This is the best part. Enjoy it.
  6. By now, some coffee will have dripped into your mug. Don't worry, refill the Aeropress to the top.
  7. Insert the plunger at the top again, and let it all sit for 1 minute again.
  8. Plunge and drink.
