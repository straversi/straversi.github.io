Baby You're a Firework

by Steven Traversi and Carlos Flores

CS184 Final Project Proposal

Summary

In this project we will try to simulate fireworks in virtual reality. We will be developing on an Oculus Rift with Unity.

Problem Description

We will try to develop a model for simulating a realistic exploding firework. Our model will focus on particle physics. The first major component of the project will be creating a particle simulator tailored to simulating fireworks. This will involve gravity, air resistance forces, momentum transfer, and the ability to change particle colors. The second major component will be creating custom behaviors for the particles that will create the image of beautiful fireworks. This may include custom particle trail behaviors, for example.

Goals and Deliverables

What we plan to deliver

We plan to deliver a working firework simulator with the following components implemented:

  • [1, 3] particle physics (general particle behavior)
  • [2] momentum transfer (explosion)
  • [1, 3] realistic trajectories of particle trails
  • [4] realistic particle decomposition (firework dying)

We imagine that it will be difficult to quantitatively analyze the correctness of the simulation. We may have to rely on visual accounts of third parties to collect unbiased opinion of accuracy.
With regard to the particle simulator: it should be possible to do an error analysis between the smooth projected path and the actual path our particles take. Simulations are time-step based and therefore produce error in the paths.

What we hope to deliver

We hope to deliver components that will improve the realism of the simulated fireworks. These may include:

  • more advanced particle trails (twirling behavior, for example)
  • sounds
  • method of toggling live simulation (interaction)

Schedule

Weekly Tasks

  1. Get objects to appear and move in Unity using controller scripts.
  2. Finish the physics simulator and test it on small particles. Try dropping objects, launching objects, exploding objects.
  3. Get a basic particle trail working. Get particle decomposition from a probability distribution working.
  4. Fine-tune particle trails. Make adjustments in pursuit of realism. Debugging.

Resources

  • Oculus Rift DK2 system and Unity 4.
  • Papers on generating particle trails efficiently.
